About

A note on me.

Most of us, somewhere along the way, get carried away in the work. We perform. We optimize. We hit the targets. We make it work. And somewhere quietly, we stop asking the questions that don't have practical answers.

Confucius wrote, two and a half thousand years ago, that life is really simple, but we insist on making it complicated. The wise men of every century since have said roughly the same thing in different words. They warned us. They left the work for us to read. Most of us never get around to it because we're busy.

I'm Taran. I spent over a decade practicing law in Ontario, primarily on real estate transactions. I now work in real estate directly. That practice runs separately at prime-gate.ca.

At some point I stopped and looked around. Not to switch careers. Not to upskill into a new field. Just to look. To step back from the autopilot and ask what those old teachers had been pointing at for so long, and what they actually meant.

The reading sent me deeper than I expected. Marcus Aurelius. Seneca. James Allen. The Stoics. The mind, and how it operates, became the question I cannot put down. How it produces what it produces. How quietly it runs a life until we notice it. How everything else flows downstream of it.

This site is my personal page. Notes from the journey. Lessons from the reading. The small things that have moved me along the way.

I run, and I'm training for a marathon. I meditate, in the unromantic sense of sitting still. I volunteer with shelter dogs. I read old books, the kind that have outlived their authors. The hours that have shaped me most haven't been the ones I spent at a desk.

If anything here is useful, take it. If not, leave it.
