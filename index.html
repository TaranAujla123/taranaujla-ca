About

A note on me.

I’m Taran.

On January 20, 2025, after years of managing external challenges that had been eroding my internal and physical health, I made a decision. Metaphorically, I killed the old version of myself. Every useless external thing tied to that version, I let go of. I stopped. Pressed a hard reset. Cleared the noise, inside and out, and focused entirely on rebuilding from the ground up, in silence.

A year later, I’ve reached a physical and mental baseline I did not think was possible to get back to.

Before that, I was the man most high-functioning men become at some point. Grinding, pushing harder, meeting every challenge head-on with the strategy I had always been handed. The strategy most of us are handed. Solve the surface. Keep moving. Don’t stop long enough to look underneath.

Eventually I noticed it was only solving surface things. Nothing permanent. The setbacks were not the interruption to the path. They were the path.

The impediment to action advances action. What stands in the way becomes the way. Marcus Aurelius

I had to stop pretending the old way was still working. I stepped back, all the way back. Before I could rebuild the outside, I had to rebuild the inside. I started with the mind, because the mind is what runs everything. I changed how I thought, what I believed, what I gave weight to, and what I let go of. The full version of what came of that is on a separate page.

What I value most from the rebuild is the composure. Less shakes me now. Problems arrive and I meet them from a steadier place. The noise underneath got quieter. That is not something I read about. It came out of the last eighteen months of doing the work.

I am not writing this from the top of anything. I am still learning, still adjusting, and that is the point.

Currently

What I’m doing now.

Three things, held together by the same idea.

The writing. I write at this site — Off-Curriculum essays on what formal education tends to leave out. The mind, the patterns underneath, what old books still teach us. Currently working on my first book: The Two Fears — on how fear of losing something and fear of not getting something quietly run most men’s lives.

Alfa Rebuild. A structured program for high-functioning men ready to rebuild their baseline — body, mind, and daily practice. Same protocol I ran on myself at 40. Details at alfarebuild.com.

Real estate. First (and still) as an investor. Was a real estate lawyer for over a decade, on the analytical and disputes side. Now on the broker side through Primegate and Resolve, because the strategy, negotiation, and deal architecture is the part I find most energizing and rewarding.

Each of these takes what the last one taught me and pushes it further.

Practice

Reading and practice.

Since the rebuild, I train. I learn. I live consciously, deliberately. I question things and take nothing on face value. I read and study Stoic philosophy, and the writers who came after who kept pulling on the same thread. I sit still often, and intentionally seek challenges, because the challenges are what forged the rebuild. I spend time with my dogs — they’ve shown me what loyalty and being real actually look like.

I don’t share this to posture or to sell. I share it to remind myself what is possible, and because a few men who read it will recognize themselves in it.

Writing this down where anyone can read it is part of the work itself. The old script has gravity. It pulls you back the moment attention slips. Making this public is what keeps me from drifting into it again.

The unexamined life is not worth living. Socrates

If anything here is useful, take it. If not, leave it.
